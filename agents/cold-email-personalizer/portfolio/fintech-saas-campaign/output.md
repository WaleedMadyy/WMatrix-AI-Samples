## 8. SEQUENCE STRATEGY NOTES

### Narrative Architecture Across 7 Touchpoints

This sequence is built as a **multi-channel persuasion arc** where each touchpoint stands alone as independently valuable, but together they construct an escalating case across two channels that becomes progressively harder to ignore.

**The Core Strategy: Surround Without Suffocate**

Email builds the intellectual case. LinkedIn builds the personal familiarity. By Day 7, David has encountered Alex Reyes in his inbox (3 emails), his LinkedIn notifications (connection request), and his LinkedIn messages (voice note) — but each interaction was respectful, substantive, and non-repetitive. Alex feels like someone in David's professional orbit, not a stranger cold-pitching.

### Touchpoint-by-Touchpoint Arc

**Email 1 — Day 0 (Relevance):** Establishes that this is not a template. The dual hiring signal proves real research and reframes the conversation from "buy our product" to "you are hiring humans to do a machine's job." The case study is introduced as a mirror — same industry, similar size, same problem — so David sees himself in the result without being pitched.

**LinkedIn Connection — Day 1 (Familiarity):** Pure social proof and visibility play. No pitch. No product. Just a peer-level connection request that references Meridian's growth. When David sees the notification, "Alex Reyes" becomes a name he has now seen twice in 24 hours across two channels. This primes recognition for every subsequent touchpoint.

**Email 2 — Day 3 (Quantification):** Shifts from narrative to math. The "4-6 touches per invoice" framework lets David calculate his own pain without being told he has a problem. At 2,000 invoices, the 10,000 monthly touchpoints figure is viscerally large. The 85% vs. 40-50% auto-match comparison is Paystream's core technical differentiator, delivered as a benchmark rather than a feature claim.

**LinkedIn Voice Note — Day 5 (Human Signal):** The highest-impact touchpoint in the sequence. Voice notes are rare in B2B outreach, which makes them stand out. Hearing Alex's actual voice transforms the interaction from "automated sales sequence" to "real person who knows my situation." The 41-word script is intentionally casual and low-pressure. The [pause] delivery notes ensure it sounds like a genuine aside, not a read script. The "not trying to chase you down" line names the elephant in the room — David knows this is a sales sequence — and the acknowledgment builds trust.

**Email 3 — Day 7 (Objection Removal):** This email exists because David's most likely internal response to the first four touchpoints is "interesting, but not now — we are too busy scaling to evaluate vendors." Email 3 names that objection explicitly, then destroys it: QuickBooks compatibility (no migration), six-week implementation (not six months), and no parallel systems. Naming QuickBooks Enterprise specifically signals stack-level research and removes the "will it even work" friction before David raises it.

**LinkedIn Comment — Day 10 (Ambient Presence):** The subtlest touchpoint. Alex engages with David's content as a thoughtful peer — no pitch, no product mention. This does two things: (1) puts Alex's name back in David's notifications without sending another email, and (2) demonstrates that Alex is a real professional who participates in industry conversations, not a bot running a sequence. If David has accepted the connection request, he sees Alex's profile (which should mention Paystream) and can self-discover the product.

**Email 4 — Day 14 (Reframe + Exit):** The breakup email introduces a completely new ROI dimension — captured early payment discounts — that David almost certainly has not quantified. Finance leaders instinctively react to money being left on the table. The 4% to 38% improvement and the $95K figure are concrete enough to stick in his memory. The breakup framing ("last note," "down the road") eliminates all pressure and paradoxically increases reply rates by 40-60% versus standard follow-ups. Even if David does not reply, this email positions Alex as someone who surfaces useful financial insights rather than chasing meetings.
